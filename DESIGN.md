## Brand & Style

The brand personality is rooted in reliability and effortless utility. It seeks to demystify the stress of urban parking through a "Grandma-friendly" lens—meaning the interface is exceptionally legible, cognitively light, and physically easy to interact with, even in high-glare outdoor environments.

The design style is **Modern Corporate with Tactile Affordance**. It leverages heavy whitespace and a limited color palette to maintain focus, while using soft, dimensional elements to signify interactivity. The goal is to evoke a sense of calm and competence, moving away from "techy" complexity toward a warm, human-centric service experience.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by a high-contrast **Trust Blue** (#1D4ED8) to ensure WCAG AA/AAA compliance for all primary actions. The functional logic is color-coded for instant recognition:
- **Success Green:** Indicates high availability ("Plenty").
- **Warning Orange:** Indicates low availability ("Limited").
- **Muted Gray:** Indicates no availability ("Full").

The background uses a soft off-white to reduce screen glare, while the primary text uses a deep navy-black to maximize readability against the light background.

## Typography

This design system uses **Plus Jakarta Sans** for its friendly, open counters and excellent legibility. The type scale is intentionally oversized to accommodate users in various lighting conditions or those with minor visual impairments. 

A clear hierarchy is maintained by using heavy font weights (Bold/ExtraBold) for headlines to anchor the page, while body text uses Medium weights to ensure stroke clarity on high-resolution mobile displays. Letter spacing is slightly tightened on headlines for a premium editorial feel, while labels remain airy for quick scanning.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout follows a **Mobile-First Fluid Grid** philosophy. On mobile devices, a 4-column system is used with 16px margins. On desktop, this expands to a 12-column fixed grid (max-width 1280px).

**Large Touch Targets:** A strict minimum hit area of 48x48px is enforced for all interactive elements.
**Generous Padding:** Containers and cards use a minimum internal padding of 20px (md) to prevent the UI from feeling cramped. Vertical rhythm is established using an 8px base unit, favoring larger gaps (lg and xl) between distinct content sections to reduce cognitive load.

## Elevation & Depth

Visual hierarchy is established through **Tonal Layering** and **Ambient Shadows**. 

1.  **Base Layer:** The light background (#F8FAFC).
2.  **Surface Layer:** Cards and containers use white backgrounds with a subtle, 1px stroke in a lightened version of the neutral color to define boundaries.
3.  **Elevated Layer:** Active or primary cards use a soft, diffused shadow (Blur: 20px, Y: 8px, Opacity: 6% Black) to "lift" them off the page, signaling they are tappable.

Avoid harsh, high-opacity shadows. Depth should feel like natural sunlight, subtle and soft.

## Shapes

The shape language is defined by **rounded-2xl** (1rem / 16px) as the standard for cards and major containers. This softness removes the "clinical" feel of many utility apps, making the experience feel more approachable.

Buttons and input fields utilize the same 16px radius. Chips and status indicators use a "Pill-shape" (full rounding) to differentiate them from functional inputs and structural cards.

## Components

### Buttons (Tactile & Inviting)
Buttons must feel like physical objects waiting to be pressed. 
- **Typography:** Use `SemiBold` or `Bold` (never ExtraBold) to maintain elegance.
- **Sizing:** Standardize to 48px or 52px height for mobile—substantial, but not aggressively bulky.
- **Styling:** Instead of a flat block of color, apply a very subtle inner highlight (e.g., a 1px top inner border of pure white at 20% opacity) and a soft drop shadow (`shadow-md shadow-blue-500/30` in Tailwind) to give it life and dimension. 

### Input Fields (Soft Focus)
Inputs should feel smooth and frictionless.
- **Resting State:** A subtle, light-gray background (`bg-slate-50`) with a delicate 1px border (`border-slate-200`). 
- **Active State:** Completely avoid harsh 2px solid borders. Instead, use a glowing focus ring (`focus:ring-2 focus:ring-blue-500/40 focus:border-blue-500`). This transition makes the UI feel modern and responsive.

### Cards (Floating Surfaces)
Cards should not look like boxes drawn on a screen; they should look like floating surfaces.
- **Styling:** Remove hard structural borders. Rely entirely on a clean white background (`bg-white`) combined with an ambient, large-spread shadow (`shadow-xl shadow-slate-200/50`). 
- **Layout:** Give elements room to breathe. The Availability Chip doesn't have to be pinned rigidly to the top right; it can float organically above the price or next to the title.

### Chips (Availability)
Status chips should be visually soft but contextually clear.
- **Shape & Style:** Fully rounded (pill shape) with a pastel background (`bg-green-100`) and a bold, darker text (`text-green-800`). Avoid heavy borders on chips.

### List Items (Spacial Separation)
- **Separation:** Do NOT use hard horizontal line dividers (`border-b`). Instead, separate list items using generous vertical whitespace (gap) or very subtle alternating background tints. This makes scrolling feel fluid rather than rigid.
